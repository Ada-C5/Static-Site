Photography

Mystical Forest

Kneeling on damp moss and decayed leaves, left cheek inches from the ground, squinting through the camera…I had a moment. I was seeing the world in vivid detail—every shade of green on a velvety frond of moss, the shape a bug’s bite makes on a lacy, dry leaf, the stumbling yet determined path a termite takes on a pulpy, rotting tree trunk—details that I normally would’ve missed on a simple stroll through the forest.

...what else do I miss, everywhere, always?

Hydrophobic

Who knew something as simple as oil in water could be so fascinating and lovely? I had fun exploring the vastly different appearance of oil in water, water in oil, and oil + detergent in water.

Salt & Sand

It was a frigid, grey day. I had little hope of finding something interesting in the low tide. So many empty husks of barnacles, so much slick brown algae covering every surface. But wait! Did that green anemone have yellow and pink tentacles? Was that giant blue barnacle actually alive? Wasn’t that barnacle graveyard hauntingly sad, but beautiful?

It was a frigid, grey, magnificent day.
